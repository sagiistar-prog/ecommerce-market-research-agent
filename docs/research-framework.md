# Research Framework

## Research Goal

The agent helps convert early product and channel assumptions into a structured market research artifact. It is optimized for cross-border ecommerce teams that need to connect research with content testing.

## Core Questions

1. What buyer jobs does this category serve?
2. Which competitor positions are already crowded?
3. What price-band expectations should be tested?
4. Which content hooks match likely buyer motivations?
5. Which claims require stronger evidence before launch?

## Source Priority

The intended source priority is:

1. User-provided product brief and constraints.
2. Internal approved research exports.
3. Public product pages or marketplace pages reviewed by a human.
4. Social and creator content samples reviewed by a human.
5. Model-generated assumptions clearly labeled as hypotheses.

The Safe Demo only uses level 1 demo fixtures.

## Evidence Levels

| Level | Meaning | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| E0 | Fictional demo fixture | Safe Demo only |
| E1 | User-provided internal note or export | Can inform draft report |
| E2 | Reviewed public source | Can support low-risk observations |
| E3 | Multi-source corroborated evidence | Can support stronger recommendations |
| E4 | Unverified assumption | Must be labeled as hypothesis |

## Output Principles

- Keep facts, assumptions, and recommendations separate.
- Label demo data as fictional.
- Attach evidence levels to claims where possible.
- Convert research into content ideas without turning hypotheses into proof.
- Add a human review prompt for risky claims.

## Content Growth Translation

Research is translated into:

- Persona-specific hooks.
- Listing and landing page copy angles.
- Creator brief prompts.
- Objection handling.
- Testable message pillars.

Each content idea should include what evidence is needed before the claim is used publicly.
