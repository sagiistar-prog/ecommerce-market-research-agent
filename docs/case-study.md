# Case Study

## Context

Cross-border ecommerce operators often need early market judgment before they have a full research budget. The first useful artifact is usually not a large dashboard. It is a structured report that helps the team decide what to test, what to say, and what to verify before launch.

This portfolio project demonstrates an AI market research agent that works from a product brief and a fictional competitor table. It produces a market research report that is useful for merchandising, channel planning, creator briefs, and content growth.

## Problem

Teams moving into a new country or channel face several recurring problems:

- Product notes, competitor observations, and content ideas are scattered.
- Evidence quality is unclear.
- Content teams need hooks quickly, but claims may be risky.
- Early competitor matrices are often informal and hard to reuse.
- Human reviewers need a checklist that is specific enough to catch risky claims.

## Solution

The agent converts local demo inputs into:

- A market context summary.
- A competitor matrix.
- Price-band interpretation.
- Buyer personas.
- Content angles.
- Messaging pillars.
- Risk prompts.
- Human review checklist.

The Safe Demo intentionally uses fictional data so the repository can be shared publicly without exposing customer, marketplace, or brand information.

## Example Scenario

The included sample brief describes a fictional product category: modular desk glow bars for small workspaces. The competitor table contains synthetic brands and synthetic positioning notes.

The generated report shows how the agent would frame a test market without pretending that the sample numbers are real market facts.

## Portfolio Strengths

- Shows product thinking, not only script execution.
- Connects research output to content growth workflows.
- Makes evidence quality explicit.
- Includes a local audit script for portfolio hygiene.
- Avoids live scraping and private data.

## Limitations

- The Safe Demo does not estimate real market size.
- Competitor data is synthetic.
- Recommendations are hypotheses for testing, not final business advice.
- Real deployment would require reviewed data connectors, source logging, and human approval gates.
